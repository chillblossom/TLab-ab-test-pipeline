What did you discover about your two datasets? Which group has the highest viewing time? What would you want to investigate next?

Which group had the highest viewing time?
The treatment group had the higher viewing times. The mean viewing time for the treatment group was 16.80 minutes compared to 12.45 minutes for the control group. There is a difference of 4.35 minutes. This suggests the new slides, 15 words/slide, kept employees engaged better than the original, 50 words/slide.

What about dispersion?
The control group was more spread out with a standard deviation of 3.26 minutes, while the treatment group was tighter at 2.09 minutes. This means:

Control group viewers were more inconsistent. Some lost interest quickly, others stayed longer.
Treatment group viewers behaved more consistently. The concise slides had more consistent engagement across the board

What do the outliers tell us?
The control group's outliers ranged from around 3.69 to 21.21 minutes, reflecting high variables, some employees barely engaged while others stayed much longer. 
The treatment group's outliers ranged from roughly 10.33 to 22.85 minutes. Higher overall, which indicates that even the low end of the treatment group outperformed much of the control group.
